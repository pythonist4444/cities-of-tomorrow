This project was developed as part of the **Microsoft Fabric Data Days Notebooks Contest** under the theme **"Cities of Tomorrow ‚Äì Urban Growth & Sustainability."** It explores the relationship between urban density, sustainability, and livability, challenging the common misconception that high population density leads to low sustainability. ## Project Objectives

1. **Data Preparation:** Clean and engineer features such as `Eco_Density_Ratio`.
2. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):** Visualize the trade-offs between urban growth and green space.
3. **Predictive Modeling:** Use machine learning to identify the top drivers of the `Urban Sustainability Score`.
4. **Clustering:** Segment cities into archetypes to guide urban planning and policy recommendations.

---

## Key Findings

1. **Density is Not Destiny:** Population density has a negligible impact on urban sustainability.
2. **The Green Imperative:** Green cover percentage is the most critical predictor of a livable city, contributing ~47% to sustainability outcomes.
3. **City Archetypes:** Using K-Means clustering, cities were grouped into three distinct clusters:
   - **Cluster 0:** High disaster risk, high crime rates, and low sustainability.
   - **Cluster 1:** Moderate sustainability, with low crime but lacking green cover and renewable energy.
   - **Cluster 2:** High sustainability, leveraging green spaces and renewable energy.
